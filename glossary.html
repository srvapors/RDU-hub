Glossary of Terms

This glossary provides definitions for the core concepts of the Resonant Dark Universe model.

Resonant Dark Universe (RDU)
A comprehensive cosmological model intended to replace the standard Lambda-CDM model. [span_0](start_span)The name describes its core principles: "Resonant" for the universal concept that everything resonates at a fundamental frequency, and "Dark" for its physical explanation of the 95% of the universe that is dark matter and dark energy[span_0](end_span).
Aumons
[span_1](start_span)
A spectrum of stable, superluminal particles that constitute the dark sector (dark matter and dark energy)[span_1](end_span). [span_2](start_span)[span_3](start_span)Aumons are the native, fundamental quanta of the Unified Field and are created when baryonic matter is transformed inside Dark Resonator Cores[span_2](end_span)[span_3](end_span).
Chronos Field ($\Psi$)
[span_4](start_span)[span_5](start_span)[span_6](start_span)

[span_7](start_span)[span_8](start_span)[span_9](start_span)Its potential drives cosmic expansion, and all fundamental particles of the Standard Model emerge as stable, resonant excitations (solitons) of this field[span_7](end_span)[span_8](end_span)[span_9](end_span).
Dark Resonator Cores (DRCs)
[span_10](start_span)[span_11](start_span)

[span_12](start_span)[span_13](start_span)A foundational axiom of the RDU, the Black Hole-DRC Identity, posits that all black holes are active DRCs[span_12](end_span)[span_13](end_span).
Gravitational Lattice
[span_14](start_span)[span_15](start_span)

[span_18](start_span)[span_19](start_span)
