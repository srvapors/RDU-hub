New Paper: How JWST's 'Impossible' Galaxies Confirm the RDU

Posted on August 24, 2025

I'm thrilled to announce the publication of my latest paper, which connects the Resonant Dark Universe directly to one of the most exciting discoveries in modern astronomy.

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) has found massive, mature galaxies in the early universe where they shouldn't exist, creating what some are calling a 'crisis' for the standard model of cosmology. These "impossible" galaxies are too big, too well-formed, and appeared too early.

My new paper details how these galaxies are not a crisis at all, but a direct confirmation of a key prediction of the Resonant Dark Universe. The RDU's two-stage formation model—using the "Gravitational Lattice" as a cosmic scaffold and Dark Resonator Cores as super-charged engines of creation—perfectly explains how these massive structures could form so rapidly.
